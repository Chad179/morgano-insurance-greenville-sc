South Carolina Auto Insurance Requirements

Every vehicle registered in South Carolina must carry liability insurance with minimum coverage of $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $25,000 for property damage. These minimums satisfy the law but often leave drivers underinsured in a serious accident on I-85 or I-385.

Uninsured motorist coverage (UM/UIM) is offered on every auto policy in South Carolina. While you can reject it in writing, most insurance agents in Greenville recommend carrying it because roughly 9% of SC drivers are uninsured. Collision and comprehensive coverage protect your own vehicle and are required by lenders if you have a car loan.

The Morgano Agency compares auto insurance rates from multiple carriers for Greenville drivers. Whether you have a clean driving record or need an SR-22 filing after a DUI, the agency finds the most competitive rate available.

Homeowners Insurance in the Greenville Area

Standard homeowners insurance in South Carolina is an HO-3 policy that covers dwelling, other structures, personal property, liability, and additional living expenses. Greenville County homes near the Reedy River, Saluda River, or Enoree River may need separate flood insurance since standard policies exclude flood damage.

The replacement cost of your home drives your homeowners insurance premium. Construction costs in Greenville have risen, and many homeowners are underinsured because their policy has not kept up with rebuilding costs. An annual policy review from an independent agent catches this gap before a claim exposes it.

Why Greenville SC Residents Choose Independent Insurance Agents

Independent insurance agents in Greenville represent multiple insurance carriers rather than a single company. This structure allows them to compare coverage options and pricing across carriers to find the best fit for each client. Unlike captive agents who can only sell one company's products, an independent agent can move your policy to a different carrier at renewal if a better rate or coverage option becomes available. This flexibility is especially valuable in South Carolina, where insurance rates vary significantly between carriers for the same coverage.

Insurance Coverage for Greenville Small Businesses

Small businesses in Greenville need a combination of coverages tailored to their industry and operations. A general liability policy protects against third-party bodily injury and property damage claims. Commercial property insurance covers your business equipment, inventory, and furnishings. Workers compensation is required for businesses with four or more employees in South Carolina. A business owners policy (BOP) bundles general liability and commercial property at a discount. Professional service firms also need errors and omissions coverage. An independent agent builds a coverage package that matches your specific business risks.
